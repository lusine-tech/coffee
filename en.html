Start your day with a sip of unique Armenian coffee

ARMENIAN COFFEE

It's hard to find a more typical "Armenian" drink than coffee. Wherever you go in Armenia, the first thing that you will be offered is definitely coffee irrespective of the time of day. Coffee is considered to be the "drink of Gods". It refreshes the body and the spirit, protects from many diseases and gives you a good mood. Coffee is a great opportunity for a nice chat. In Armenia coffee is made with a special recipe. First, coffee beans are lightly fried on a warmed frying pan, then grinded into powder. After that you put a spoon of coffee in the coffee maker, sugar depending on taste and a cup of water. Be sure not to boil the coffee! As soon as the foam is visible take it off the gas stone before it starts boiling. Sometimes people add cognac to coffee to get a special taste bouquet.

Armenia is one of the coffee centers of the world

In the XIX century Armenian refugees arrived in Colombia and set up an Armenian colony and called it Armenia. The world's most famous coffee museum is now in that city. It's not accidental, as it's in the province of Quindio, which is the leading producer of aromatic coffee in Colombia.

Armenians were the first nation to bring coffee to Europe

According to historians it was Armenian merchants who brought coffee to Europe in the XVI century. In the second half of the XVII century, an Armenian merchant named Harutyun opened the first coffee booth in Paris. Later, his compatriot opened a cafe there. Soon, Parisian coffee spread to other European cities as well.
